{# General Advice Section #}

General Health Recommendations

Regardless of your risk level, maintaining a healthy lifestyle is crucial for preventing and managing diabetes. Here are some general guidelines:

{# Diet Section #}

Dietary Guidelines

  • Balanced Meals: Focus on whole foods, including plenty of vegetables, fruits, lean proteins, and whole grains.
  • Limit Sugars: Reduce intake of sugary drinks, processed foods, and refined carbohydrates.
  • Healthy Fats: Choose healthy fats from sources like avocados, nuts, seeds, and olive oil.
  • Portion Control: Be mindful of portion sizes to manage calorie intake.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of water throughout the day.
{% if diabetes_risk == 'HIGH' %}

High Risk Specific: Strictly adhere to a low-glycemic diet. Consider consulting a registered dietitian for a personalized meal plan.

{% elif diabetes_risk == 'MEDIUM' %}

Medium Risk Specific: Pay close attention to carbohydrate intake and choose complex carbs. Limit saturated and trans fats.

{% endif %}
{# Lifestyle Section #}

Lifestyle Adjustments

  • Stress Management: Practice stress-reducing techniques like meditation, yoga, or deep breathing.
  • Quit Smoking: If you smoke, quitting is one of the most impactful changes you can make.
  • Limit Alcohol: Consume alcohol in moderation, if at all.
  • Regular Check-ups: Schedule regular health check-ups with your doctor.
{% if diabetes_risk == 'HIGH' %}

High Risk Specific: Implement significant lifestyle changes immediately. Seek professional guidance for comprehensive wellness planning.

{% endif %}
{# Exercise Section #}

Exercise Recommendations

  • Aerobic Activity: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week (e.g., brisk walking, cycling, swimming).
  • Strength Training: Incorporate strength training exercises at least twice a week.
  • Stay Active: Reduce sedentary time by taking short walks or stretching breaks throughout the day.
{% if diabetes_risk == 'HIGH' %}

High Risk Specific: Increase physical activity gradually under medical supervision. Consider high-intensity interval training (HIIT) if appropriate.

{% elif diabetes_risk == 'MEDIUM' %}

Medium Risk Specific: Consistently meet recommended exercise guidelines. Explore different activities to find what you enjoy.

{% endif %}
{# Sleep Section #}

Sleep Hygiene

  • Consistent Schedule: Go to bed and wake up at the same time each day, even on weekends.
  • Adequate Sleep: Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night.
  • Comfortable Environment: Ensure your bedroom is dark, quiet, and cool.
  • Limit Screens: Avoid screens (phones, tablets, TV) at least an hour before bedtime.
{% if diabetes_risk == 'HIGH' %}

High Risk Specific: Prioritize sleep and address any sleep disorders (e.g., sleep apnea) with a healthcare professional.

{% endif %}
{# Medical Details Section #}

Medical Considerations

  • Consult Your Doctor: Always discuss your risk assessment and any health concerns with a qualified medical professional.
  • Regular Monitoring: Your doctor may recommend specific tests (e.g., A1C, fasting blood glucose) for regular monitoring.
  • Medication (if applicable): Follow your doctor's advice regarding any prescribed medications.
{% if diabetes_risk == 'HIGH' %}

High Risk Specific: Immediate consultation with an endocrinologist or a doctor specializing in diabetes is highly recommended for comprehensive evaluation and management plan.

{% elif diabetes_risk == 'MEDIUM' %}

Medium Risk Specific: Discuss your risk with your primary care physician and consider more frequent screenings.

{% else %} {# LOW Risk #}

Low Risk Specific: Continue with healthy habits and regular check-ups to maintain your low risk status.

{% endif %}

*Disclaimer: These suggestions are for informational purposes only and do not constitute medical advice. Always consult with a healthcare professional for personalized guidance.*
